## What this is

A complete, ready-to-use toolkit for mapping, documenting, and managing the personal data your organisation holds — across all systems, vendors, and data types.

Built for compliance with the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)** and the draft **DPDP Rules, 2025**.

---

## Why data inventory matters under the DPDP Act

The DPDP Act does not use the phrase "data inventory" — but it is the foundational exercise that makes everything else possible.

Without knowing what data you hold, you cannot:

- Identify which processing activities require consent (Section 6)
- Respond to Data Principal access requests (Section 11)
- Respond to Data Principal correction and erasure requests (Section 12 and Section 13)
- Appoint or manage Data Fiduciary relationships with vendors (Section 8)
- Implement appropriate security safeguards (Section 8(4))
- Comply with data retention and deletion obligations (Section 8(7))
- Notify the Data Protection Board in the event of a breach (Section 8(6))

A data inventory is the map of your data estate. Everything else is built on it.

## How to use this kit

**Step 1 — Read the framework**
Start with `02-data-inventory-framework/what-is-a-data-inventory.md`. Understand what you are mapping and why.

**Step 2 — Choose your starting point**
- Smaller businesses or first-timers: start with `data-inventory-lite.csv`
- Larger businesses or those doing a full audit: start with `data-inventory-master.csv`

**Step 3 — Go sector-specific**
Open the folder in `05-sector-guides` that matches your business. The CSV is pre-filled with typical systems and data types for your sector.

**Step 4 — Map your data flows**
Use `04-data-flow-diagrams/data-flow-template.html` to visualise how data moves through your organisation.

**Step 5 — Use your inventory to drive compliance**
Your completed inventory feeds directly into your consent notices, vendor agreements, privacy policy, and breach response plan.
